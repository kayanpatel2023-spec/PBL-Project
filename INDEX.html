Fashion Trend Determination: Generational Aesthetics & Emotional Marketing

This research analyzes how generational aesthetics, seasonal changes, and psychological marketing strategies influence fashion consumption behavior.

Problem Statement

Fashion consumption today is increasingly influenced by emotional triggers and promotional pricing strategies rather than actual need. There is limited integrated research examining how generational identity, seasonal variation, and behavioral marketing techniques collectively shape consumer purchasing decisions.

Literature Review

Existing studies highlight fashion diffusion theory, generational identity formation, consumer behavior models, and the role of social media in accelerating trend adoption.

Research Gap

Most studies analyze emotional buying, generational aesthetics, or seasonal fashion independently. This study integrates all three within a unified analytical framework.

Research Methodology

Data Collection

Survey conducted among Gen Z and Millennials regarding fashion preferences, seasonal choices, and response to promotional offers.

Data Analysis

Descriptive analysis using percentage comparison to identify behavioral patterns and emotional influence.

Results & Analysis

Impulse Buying Rate 72%

Results indicate that promotional marketing strategies significantly increase impulse purchases. Younger consumers show stronger emotional response to urgency-based pricing schemes.

Conclusion

Fashion trends are shaped not only by aesthetics and seasonal shifts but also by emotional triggers and strategic marketing techniques. Consumer behavior is largely influenced by psychological pricing mechanisms.
